About

I grew up on the beautiful coast of Southern California, just outside of Los Angeles. In 2015, I graduated from Redondo Union High School and went on to study business part-time at Long Beach Community College. As a college student, I worked as a restaurant server and traveled most of my summers doing volunteer work.

Fast forward to 2020. Like many in my community, my job was affected by the pandemic shutdown, and in June 2020, I graduated with my Associate's Degree in Business Administration.

Following the loss of my serving job, I saw this unique period of time as an opportunity to expand my skillset and prepare for my next career move. I have always been intrigued by programming and was seriously considering transfering to university and completing by Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science...

However, after careful research, I decided that a bootcamp may be the best option to build the foundation for my coding knowledge. I began studying full-time and was very quickly hooked - it was like an entirely new world had just opened up. I loved the blend of logic, creativity, and problem solving and couldnâ€™t wait to learn more! While attending bootcamp online, I supplemented my learning with personal projects/practice and self-study.

At one point I couldn't help but regret not pursuing this career earlier. That said, I am actually very proud of the journey. I believe that my extensive experience in customer service, as well as my education in business administration will make me a more well-rounded employee in any position. My end goal is to work remotely in a developer role. I am eager to put the hours and the effort into achieving that goal :)

When I am not learning/practicing my code or working on an awesome new project, I am probably:

Meeting new people or trying something new / At the beach / Going for a hike or a drive / Exploring new LA restaurants, bars, or music events (when it is safe to do so of course) / Writing / Traveling or road-tripping / Karaoke(ing?) / Chatting w/ a friend about movies.
